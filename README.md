 Myntra Product Intelligence Dashboard (End-to-End Data Analytics Project)

📌 Project Overview

This project is an end-to-end data analytics case study on a large Myntra fashion product dataset (~526,000 products). The goal was to analyze product performance, pricing strategies, and customer engagement to generate actionable business insights.

The project follows a complete analytics pipeline:

- Data Cleaning & Exploration (Python)
- Business Analysis (SQL)
- Visualization & Dashboarding (Power BI)

---

🎯 Business Objective

- Understand product and category performance in an online marketplace
- Analyze pricing and discount strategies
- Identify factors influencing customer engagement (ratings & reviews)
- Detect brand dominance and supply concentration

---

📊 Dataset Information

- Source: Myntra Fashion Product Dataset (Kaggle)
- Size: 526K+ products
- Key Challenge: 300K+ missing values in ratings & reviews

---

🛠️ Tools & Technologies

- Python (Pandas, NumPy)
- SQL (MySQL)
- Power BI
- Excel

---

🔧 Phase 1: Data Cleaning & Exploration (Python)

- Removed irrelevant columns (URLs, descriptions, etc.)
- Handled 300K+ missing values
- Fixed inconsistencies in pricing (original vs discounted price)
- Created a new feature: DiscountPercent
- Removed duplicates and ensured data consistency
- Performed EDA (price distribution, outlier detection, correlation analysis)

---

🧠 Phase 2: Data Analysis (SQL)

- Analyzed category-wise product distribution
- Identified brand dominance and inventory share
- Compared discount strategies across categories
- Evaluated relationship between price, discount, and ratings
- Measured customer engagement using ratings & review counts
- Ranked brands using SQL window functions

---

📊 Phase 3: Dashboard (Power BI)

An interactive dashboard was built to visualize key business metrics:

Key Features:

- KPIs: Total Products, Brands, Avg Ratings, Discount Trends
- Category-wise product distribution
- Brand dominance analysis
- Price vs Rating relationship
- Customer engagement tracking

---

📈 Key Insights

- Western Wear and Indian Wear drive the highest engagement
- A small number of brands dominate the marketplace inventory
- Higher discounts do not guarantee better ratings
- Product ratings are consistently stable (~4.1–4.2) across categories
- Price has minimal impact on customer ratings
- Premium segments are controlled by fewer, strong brands

---

💡 Business Implications

- Marketplace is highly concentrated → opportunity for niche categories
- Discounts alone are not effective → quality & brand trust matter
- Stable ratings indicate standardized customer expectations
- High-performing categories should be prioritized for growth
- Strong brands can leverage premium positioning
